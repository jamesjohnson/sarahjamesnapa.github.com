How can you visit the Napa Valley and not do any wine tasting? We have had the best time wine tasting on our many visits, and wanted to suggest some of our favorite spots. The good news is that you can't go wrong, we had fun everywhere we visited.

  • Ma(i)sonry* - A gorgeous spot which is both an art gallery and tasting room. They showcase a rotating collection of wines from many different wineries and vintners. Our Favorite!
  • Domaine Chandon - We highly recommend stopping here on your drive in for some bubbles!
  • Hall Winery in St. Helena - Cool tasting room and great wines makes this spot a no brainer to visit. If you are a pinot lover check out there WALT Pinot.
  • Robert Sinskey in Napa Great wine, beautiful vineyard and a place we love. You can even feed their fish while you are there.

Here are also a few links to check out that have more information:

  • Napa Valley Wineries

The only thing possibly better than wine tasting in Napa is the food. WOW. We can't fit all the amazing spots on this page, but we included some of the highlights. They aren't all necessarily "affordable", but they are worth it!

  • Redd - We had an amazing dinner here and enjoyed sitting outside by the outdoor fireplace. Located in Yountville.
  • Auberge du Soleil - Fancy but an incredible meal with breathtaking views. They also have a more casual grill restaurant or just stop in for a drink!
  • Bouchon - A more affordabale Thomas Keller option, great for lunch or brunch.
  • JoLē - Dining in Calistoga, haven't been but have heard great things.
  • Solbar - Amazing food, fun atmosphere, we dined outside and can't wait to return! Highly recommend.
  • Oakville Grocery - Sandwiches to-go, really fun grocery ang great place to pick up prepared food, sandwiches and cheese.
  • Tra Vigne - Welcome party is here, but great anytime of day and their hand pulled mozzarella is a must.
  • Model Bakery - Bomb.com breakfast.
  • Goose & Gander and ArchetypeBoth places have amazing cocktails!!

Napa has beautiful weather most of the year, but especially in May(fingers crossed!). There should be some good opportunities to hit the outdoors, and we thought of a few things you could do during your stay:

  • Yoga - The bride-to-be will be getting her OM on.
  • Golf - We haven't played there, but imagine its beautiful.
  • Hiking - Mt. St. Helena is the highest peak in the San Francisco watershed and the hike is five miles long to the summit
  • Picnics - Swing by the Oakville Grocery, grab some food and head to V. Sattui!
